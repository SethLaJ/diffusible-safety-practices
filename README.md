# Diffusible Safety Practices

An interactive scoring tool that applies Everett Rogers' *Diffusion of Innovations* 
framework to 75 road safety practices, evaluating each across five adoption attributes: 
relative advantage, trialability, observability, compatibility, and complexity.

Practices are scored from the perspective of a mid-size city department of transportation 
engineer with Vision Zero exposure — the adopter population most strategically positioned 
to seed transformational safety practice in the United States.

The analysis draws on prior work mapping the power and influence structure of U.S. 
transportation engineering and a Centola-informed sociometric analysis of where complex 
contagions of safety practice are most likely to take hold.

## Background

Developed by Seth LaJeunesse, Senior Researcher, 
[UNC Highway Safety Research Center](https://hsrc.unc.edu), 
in collaboration with Claude (Anthropic).

The 75 practices are drawn from a road safety practice inventory developed for 
applied research and professional development purposes.

## Scoring methodology

Each practice is scored 1–5 on five Rogers attributes. Complexity is inverted 
(5 = low complexity = favorable for adoption). The composite score out of 25 
determines priority tier:

- **Seed first** — score ≥ 20
- **Seed second** — score 15–19  
- **Longer horizon** — score ≤ 14

Scores reflect perceived attributes from the target adopter's perspective, 
not objective properties of the practice itself.
